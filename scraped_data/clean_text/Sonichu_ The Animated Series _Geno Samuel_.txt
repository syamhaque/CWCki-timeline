Sonichu: The Animated Series (no relation to the prior series by Spazkid) is a cancelled animated series by Geno Samuel which infringed on Chris's work and was taken down by YouTube after Chris submitted a copyright complaint. The trailer and two episodes had been uploaded at the time. Chris later lifted the complaint after talking with Geno.[1]
The series was directly based on Chris's comic. Several voice actors were recruited (it is unclear whether or not they were paid) to read lines straight from the comic. It heavily used Chris's artwork and applied motion effects and limited animation to it, but without his permission. Geno monetized the project through his Patreon.[2]
On 7 December 2024, Geno posted a short video of himself editing the beginning of the first episode.[3] On 13 April 2025, Geno posted a trailer for the series (the series intro), then on 20 April, he uploaded "Episode 1: Sonichu's Origins",[4] acknowledging Chris with "Written & Drawn by CHRISTIAN WESTON CHANDLER" in the end credits. This was followed by "Episode 2: Genesis of the Lovehogs" on 3 May.[5]
Chris then made a copyright removal request to YouTube due to the videos using content from his comic Sonichu 0, which was granted and resulted in the first two episodes, along with the trailer intro video, being taken down and a copyright strike placed on Geno's channel on 5 May 2025. Geno has commented that Chris had a case due to him using Chris's artwork, and he claimed the series would not be continuing, noting episode 3 was finished, 4 nearly so, and voicework had been completed through 6.[6] A few hours later, Chris made a statement confirming the legitimacy of the strike:[7]
