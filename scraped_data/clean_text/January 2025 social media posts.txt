Thoughts on Artificial Intelligence
17 January
1:55 PM
Power to you, #saberspark.
I 101% agree that AI generated media is beyond subpart.
I feel and agree that human created art and content is far superior as they contain the love and soul as well as care and compassion into the media.
[Posts link to a YouTube video by Saberspark titled "This Is The WORST AI Content Farm on YouTube..."]
Not to mention how bad AI would take jobs from people.
AI is not to be constantly relied upon, because of likelihood of failure in programming.
And in media companies mistakenly firing artists and creative people for AI, you'd essentially get MLPG5 all over again and worse.
Not to mention recklessly encouraging next gen children and individuals to be really uncultured and brain-numbed; we need mentally and emotionally strong individuals inspired by authentic, high-quality, soul-filled stories and content with thought-provoking ideas and thought sets with light-powered
...encouragement and guidance.
Don't just be greedy and churning out low-quality crap; kids, teens and babies are meant to think and understand as much as possible; they are smarter than one may think.
As are we who are experienced and understanding of better quality in media and arts.
So, in a sense, "Make them better quality like they used to before 2019 as the humans do.
Not the new-fangled AI video generating garbage."

A bit of wisdom
25 January
12:25 PM
Here's a bit of wisdom for you all: Sometimes, even as teens and adults, we need to be reminded of how to do good for and with ourselves and others.
So, yes, it is good to have a favourite or new good quality affirming show playing in the background.
[Posts GIF of Vinyl Scratch and Derpy Hooves from My Little Pony.]
Also, in that, everyone is going to have at least one traumatic event in their childhood or past, which is best to give yourself and your inner child security and assurance.
Caring for yourself allows you to better care for others as well.
That's a reason behind "Me Time": to heal and process.
